        <p class="body-text">
            British-American violinist Nicholas Cerny is a second-year postgraduate violin student at the Royal College of Music, studying with Daniel Rowland.
            He recently served as Leader of the Schleswig-Holstein Musik Festival Orchestra under the direction of Maestro Christoph Eschenbach, leading performances across
            Germany and Denmark with an orchestra featuring members from over 30 countries. At the RCM, he has served as Associate Leader and Principal Second Violin of the
            RCM Symphony Orchestra and is a founding member of the Cordelia Quartet, which performs regularly across London. Prior to attending the RCM, Nicholas graduated
            <em>magna cum laude</em> from Yale University, where he simultaneously studied chemistry and music, earning distinction in both subjects as well as the YSEA Award
            for Outstanding Undergraduate Achievement and the Michael Manzella and Fleischer Prizes.
